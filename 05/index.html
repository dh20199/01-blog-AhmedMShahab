J. R. R. Tolkien

Ahmed Mustafa Shahab

J. R. R. Tolkien (full name John Ronald Reuel Tolkien) was a British writer and academic. He was born in 1892 in South Africa. He moved to England at a young age of three. Tolkien studied at Exeter College, Oxford, and was Merton professor of English Language and Literature at Oxford from 1945 to 1959.

Tolkien is most famous for writing a great number of books set in a pre-historic world based on a complex mythology of his own creation called Middle-earth. His most well known books gained international fame. The first is The Hobbit, a children’s book published in 1937 and the first book set in Tolkien’s Middle-earth. The second is a sequel story called The Lord of the Rings trilogy: Fellowship of the Ring, The Two Towers, and The Return of the King, all of which were published in 1954 to 1955. Tolkien also wrote other books like The Silmarillion (1977), Unfinished Tales of Numenor and Middle Earth (1980), and The Book of Lost Tales (1983-5) which were published after his death, and where he created stories that expanded on the world of Middle-earth. Tolkien has created a massive world in Middle-earth with its own mythology, geography, inhabitants, and languages. Tolkien’s work has been massively influential and garnered a massive following.

Though mostly famous for his novels, Tolkien also had a reputation as a scholar in Middle and Old English and published a number of philological and critical studies like A Middle-Earth Vocabulary (1922), an edition of Sir Gawain and the Green Knight (1925), and Beowulf: The Monsters and the Critics (1936).

Tolkien died in Oxford on the 2nd of September 1973, and was buried together with his wife, Edith.
